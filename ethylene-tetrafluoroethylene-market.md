<p><p>The Ethylene Tetrafluoroethylene (ETFE) market encompasses two primary processing types: ETFE injection molding and ETFE extrusion molding. ETFE injection molding involves heating the polymer and using it to fill a mold, producing complex shapes with high precision. In contrast, ETFE extrusion molding involves forcing the melted polymer through a die, creating continuous shapes like films or sheets. Both methods leverage ETFE's unique properties, such as chemical resistance and durability, to cater to diverse applications in industries like construction, electronics, and automotive.</p></p>
