<p><ul><li>Ion Exchange Chromatography</li><li>Ion Exclusion Chromatography</li><li>Ion Pair Chromatography</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Ultra High Performance Liquid Chromatography (UHPLC) market comprises several key types, each serving distinct analytical needs. Ion Exchange Chromatography is utilized for separating charged particles based on their affinity to the stationary phase. Ion Exclusion Chromatography targets ionic compounds, allowing non-ionic substances to pass through more freely. Ion Pair Chromatography combines the principles of ion exchange with a pairing agent to improve the separation of ionic and polar compounds, enhancing sensitivity and resolution in complex mixtures.</p></p>
