<p><p>Electron Spectroscopy for Chemical Analysis (ESCA) encompasses two primary types: monochromatic and non-monochromatic. Monochromatic ESCA employs a single, well-defined energy source, leading to higher resolution and more accurate chemical state identification. In contrast, non-monochromatic ESCA utilizes a broader energy spectrum, which may enhance the measurement speed but often sacrifices resolution and specificity. Both methods cater to different analytical needs in surface chemistry, materials science, and related fields, allowing researchers to choose based on resolution and data requirements.</p></p>
