<p><p>The antimicrobial fibers market is divided into two main types: natural antibacterial fibers and chemical antibacterial fibers. Natural antibacterial fibers are derived from organic sources, such as cotton, wool, or bamboo, offering antimicrobial properties through their inherent qualities. In contrast, chemical antibacterial fibers are manufactured by incorporating chemical agents during production, which enhance their ability to resist microbial growth. Both types find applications in textiles, medical products, and various industries seeking to improve hygiene and reduce infections.</p></p>
